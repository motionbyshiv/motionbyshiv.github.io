The fine print, in large print

Each tier, explained like we’re already working together.

Tier 01 — Polish · $1,495/mo

For teams who create, but drown in finishing.

Why this tier exists: founders and marketing leads record plenty — podcasts, demos, talking-head takes — and it dies in a folder because editing is the bottleneck. Polish removes that bottleneck for less than a part-time editor.

Expected outcome: a consistent publishing cadence within the first two weeks — every channel fed weekly from content you already make.

  • Deliverables: up to 20 finished edits/month — short-form verticals, cutdowns, captioned clips, channel-formatted exports
  • Timeline: first drafts within 48 working hours of starting each asset
  • Revisions: 2 rounds per asset, included
  • Milestones: day 0 onboarding → day 2 first drafts → day 7 first delivery → weekly batches after
  • Your side: raw footage or assets via shared drive, brand basics (logo, fonts, colors), one feedback owner
  • Not included: original design, animation, or concepting (that’s Animate)
  • Not included: filming or footage creation — we finish what you shoot

Tier 02 — Animate · $2,995/mo · Most chosen

For teams whose real bottleneck is original creative.

Why this tier exists: most growing teams don’t need more editing — they need creative that doesn’t exist yet: explainers, launch films, motion-led ads. Animate is a senior motion designer, concepter, and production team in one line item.

Expected outcome: a brand that looks like it has an in-house motion team — launch-grade creative on tap, with ad variants that keep paid channels fresh.

  • Deliverables: up to 10 original motion pieces/month (explainers, logo animations, motion ads, feature announcements) plus everything in Polish
  • Timeline: standard pieces drafted in 48 working hours; larger pieces (60s+ films) get a written timeline before we start
  • Revisions: 3 rounds per asset, included
  • Milestones: day 0 onboarding → day 1–2 concepts/styleframes → day 3–5 animation drafts → day 7 first delivery → monthly creative review
  • Your side: brand guidelines (or one hour to build a mini-guide with us), product access or assets, one feedback owner, feedback within 2 working days to hold timelines
  • Not included: multi-channel campaign strategy from scratch (that’s Generate)
  • Not included: media buying or ad account management

Tier 03 — Generate · $4,995/mo

For teams who want campaigns, not tasks.

Why this tier exists: at a certain scale, briefing individual assets is the bottleneck. Generate takes a one-page brief — audience, goal, channels — and returns a finished campaign: concept, script, full asset suite, every format.

Expected outcome: 1–2 complete campaigns shipped monthly, with an asset volume no shoot budget could match — at subscription cost.

  • Deliverables: 1–2 full campaigns/month — concept, scripting, hero film, cutdowns, statics, and channel variants — plus everything in Animate
  • Timeline: concept within 3 working days of brief; full campaign delivery agreed in writing at kickoff (typically 2–3 weeks)
  • Revisions: 3 rounds at each stage gate (concept → drafts → final)
  • Milestones: brief → concept approval → production drafts → final delivery — you approve at each gate, so there are no surprises at the end
  • Your side: a one-page brief per campaign, brand guidelines, approval at each gate within 2 working days
  • Not included: media buying, landing pages, or copywriting beyond the campaign assets
  • Not included: guaranteed same-week campaign turnaround (see Custom for SLAs)

Tier 04 — Custom · scoped to you

For teams where creative capacity is infrastructure.

Why this tier exists: multi-brand companies and heavy advertisers need guaranteed capacity, written SLAs, and terms their procurement team will sign. Custom is the same production line, written into a contract your procurement team will actually sign.

Expected outcome: a creative production line with a dedicated turnaround SLA — planned around your launch calendar, reported on your cadence.

  • How it starts: a scoping call, then a written proposal with volume, SLA, milestones, and pricing — typically within one week
  • Includes: quarterly strategy sessions, custom reporting, multiple brands, and contract flexibility

How billing works

Simple on purpose.

Monthly subscription, billed at the start of each cycle. No hourly billing, no per-project quotes inside your tier, no invoicing theater.

  • Start: we confirm your tier on WhatsApp, you pay month one, onboarding starts same day
  • Pause: freezes your remaining days and keeps your board intact — resume whenever
  • Cancel: one message stops the next cycle; every file is handed over
  • Upgrade / downgrade: switch tiers at any cycle boundary — we’ll recommend down as readily as up

Pricing FAQs

What counts as one deliverable?

One finished asset in one primary format — e.g. a 30s explainer, or a captioned vertical clip. Simple format variants (9:16 / 1:1 / 16:9) of the same asset count as one.

What happens to unused capacity?

Capacity is monthly and doesn’t roll over — it’s what keeps the queue fast for everyone. If you’re consistently under, we’ll suggest downgrading. Really.

Are there setup or onboarding fees?

No. Onboarding is part of month one, and the 7-day guarantee covers it.

What if we need more revisions?

Included rounds cover almost every project. If feedback keeps moving past them, we’ll flag it early and agree a small per-round rate in writing before continuing — never a silent charge.

Do you offer one-off projects?

Occasionally, when the calendar allows — message us with scope and timing. Subscriptions always get priority.
