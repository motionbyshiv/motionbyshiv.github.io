The situation

Good products, tired creative, rising CPAs.

A DTC brand had solid packshot photography and two ad concepts that had carried the account for six months. Frequency was climbing, ROAS was slipping, and the founder was quoting shoots that cost more than a quarter of ad spend.

The engagement

Animate tier, ads-focused. Onboarding collected the photo library and account data once. From week one, the loop ran weekly: a variant batch drafted from the existing photography — product motion, new hooks, seasonal angles — reviewed on the board, delivered per placement. Account screenshots came back every Monday; winners got iterations, losers got autopsies.

The result

Thirty-six variants in a quarter without a single new shoot. Creative fatigue stopped dictating the media plan, blended ROAS reached 3.2×, and the brand walked into peak season with a library of proven concepts ready to refresh.

Deliverables

  • Weekly ad variant batches (motion + static)
  • Product motion from existing packshots
  • Hook / angle testing matrix, maintained monthly
  • Every placement ratio, every batch
