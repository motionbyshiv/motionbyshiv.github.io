About the studio

Built by an artist who thinks like an engineer.

Motion By Shiv exists because of a gap we kept seeing: agencies with taste move too slowly, and tools with speed have no taste. We built a founder-led studio where both live in the same system — senior judgment on every frame, machine speed underneath — and priced it for the teams the big agencies ignore.

The founder

Shiv Sharma — 8 years of motion, a lifetime of systems.

Our studio is led by Shiv Sharma: 8 years in motion design and animation, with a computer science engineering background. That combination is the whole thesis — work that looks senior-agency-polished, shipped with the speed and systems-thinking of an engineer.

The engineering side built our AI production stack: the generation, editing, and automation tooling that lets a lean studio produce at the volume of a full team. The design side is why none of it looks automated. Every asset that leaves the studio passes senior review — the tools handle volume; the humans own taste.

Why “we”?

A studio, honestly described

We run as a studio: senior creative direction at the core, a production system engineered in-house, and specialist collaborators brought in when a project calls for them. What we never do is hand your work to a junior and disappear — the senior name on the door reviews every frame that ships.

How we operate

Four principles that show up in the work, not just on this page.

01

Speed is a feature of quality

Slow creative isn’t more considered — it’s just slower. Our stage gates put the deliberation where it’s cheap (briefs, styleframes) so production can be fast where it counts.

02

Transparency beats persuasion

Prices on the website. Timelines in writing. A guarantee with teeth. We’d rather lose a prospect to honesty than win a client with fog.

03

The work is yours

Every asset, every source file, permanently — including if you leave. Agencies that hold files hostage are financing their retention with your property.

04

AI serves taste, never replaces it

We use generative tools aggressively and admit it plainly. But if a piece looks generated, it doesn’t ship — a human with 8 years of judgment decides what’s good.
