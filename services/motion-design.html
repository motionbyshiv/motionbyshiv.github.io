How a piece gets made

You approve the look before we animate a frame.

The most expensive mistake in animation is discovering at the end that the style was wrong at the start. Our stage gates make that impossible.

The full studio process

  1. 01 · Brief & script

    We align on message and length

    A short brief you approve: audience, message, duration, references. For explainers, we script it — you sign off on every word before design starts.

  2. 02 · Styleframes

    Static frames of the final look

    Key frames designed in full fidelity. This is where taste decisions happen — cheaply and quickly, before animation.

  3. 03 · Animation

    The approved look, brought to life

    Standard pieces draft within 48 working hours; films get a written timeline at kickoff. Drafts land on your board as they render.

  4. 04 · Finish & deliver

    Sound, polish, every format

    Sound design, final QC at senior level, and delivery in every agreed aspect ratio — with 3 revision rounds included.

Common questions

Before you ask.

How long does an explainer take?

A typical 45–60 second explainer runs about two weeks from approved script to final — script and styleframes in week one, animation and finishing in week two. You’ll have the exact timeline in writing before we start.

2D, 3D, or AI-generated — what do you actually use?

Whatever serves the piece: 2D motion graphics as the backbone, 3D and generative techniques where they add production value. The style is decided at the styleframe stage, with you — and nothing that looks generated ships.

Do you handle voiceover and music?

Yes — VO casting, licensed music, and sound design are part of the finishing stage. Licensing costs are quoted transparently up front when premium tracks are involved.

Can you match our existing brand style?

That’s the default. We work inside your guidelines — and if you don’t have motion guidelines yet, the brand motion system add-on builds them once so everything after moves consistently.
